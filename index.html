Yadhira Gomez's Tips on How to write good captions in Photojournalism

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to catch the eyes of your audience

Assembly time: 30 minutes
Awesome rating: 10% awesome

Captioning photos is an important part of Journalism. Follow the steps below to write the best captions that are accurate and informative!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Camera
  • Photo Editing software
  • Social Media
  • Phone
  • Keyboard

  1. Learning the Basics of Captions.

    CHECK YOUR FACTS.Accuracy is an important part of journalism. If you use incorrect information,the story or photo will lose credibilty.Before uploading or printing any photo captions,make sure you have double checked that everything is accurate.

  2. Describe something that isn't obvious.

    If a photo caption simply describes the visuals in the photo, it becomes fairly useless. If you have a photo of a sunset and simply caption it as "a sunset" you are not adding additional information for the audience. Instead describe details,such as location, time of day, specific even that is taking place."Avoid wording such as "is shown", "is pictured", and "above".

    >

  3. Do not start a caption with certain words.

    A caption should not begin with the words 'a,''an,' or 'the'. These words are too basic and take up valuable captioning room when they are not necessary. Try avoiding starting the cpation with the name of someone.Start with a describtion then name. When Identifying where someone is in the photo, you can say "from left". You do not have to say "from left to right".

  4. Identify the main people in the photo.

    If your photo includes important people identify who they are. If you know their names, include them (unless asked to remain anonymous). If you don"t know their names, you might have to put a description of who they are instead. Make sure names are spelled correctly and have proper titles. If they photo has multiple people, or some may not be relavent to the story, you don't have to name each of of them in the caption.

  5. Be specific as possible.

    This step goes hand-and-hand with being accurate. If you are unsure of where the photo was taken, or who is in the photo, find out. If working with another Journalist, contact them for the information you need. While specific is good, you may also phrase your caption so it starts general and becomes more specific.
